## Modern Testing Challenges That Demand AI Solutions

Although software development has made strides forward, traditional test coverage approaches reflect several shortcomings:

### 1. **Restricted Functional Coverage**
Traditional strategies for test implementation often leave critical edge cases unaddressed due to human limitations and subjective assumptions about user behavior. Missed edge cases can lead to costly post-release issues.

### 2. **Sluggish Workflow**
Manual and automated testing are time-intensive, making it difficult for organizations practicing DevOps or Agile methods to match delivery speed while ensuring quality.

### 3. **High Maintenance of Automated Tests**
Automated tests frequently become brittle when software changes regularly. Maintenance burdens slow productivity and detract from innovation efforts.

### 4. **Bias in Human-Generated Tests**
Test design created by human testers often carries potential biases, limiting the diversity of test coverage and exposing software to critical vulnerabilities.

### 5. **Reactive Testing Priorities**
Without predictive insights, tests are selected based on outdated patterns or subjective analysis, leading to inefficient utilization of resources.

These constraints necessitate the adoption of AI to inject agility, accuracy, and efficiency into testing workflows.
